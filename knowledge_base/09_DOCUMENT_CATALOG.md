## `Operational_Spine_Expanded_Canonical_V1.pdf` (Pages 6–10)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **File** | `Operational_Spine_Expanded_Canonical_V1.pdf` |
| **Pages** | 10 total; this entry covers pages 6–10 |
| **Document Type** | Canonical Operational Framework |
| **Relationship to LIFE** | Core governance document — defines how every operational system in the park is governed and locked |

### Purpose
This is the back half of the park's master operational governance document. Pages 1–5 (read previously) covered systems 1–6 (Guest Flow, Animal Feeding, Interaction System, Diet Kitchen, Staff Roles, Handover/Reporting). Pages 6–10 complete the spine by governing signage, safety communication, TV/screen content, commercial clarity, email/phone, staff visibility expectations, lock state, execution order, and forward direction.

### Section-by-Section Content (Pages 6–10)

**Section 7 — Signage System Governance**
- Only four sign categories may exist: Directional, Instructional, Educational, Commercial
- Digital-first rule: dynamic information → digital screens; static only → print
- Replacement rule: any broken/confusing/off-brand/outdated sign is removed and replaced with a canonical template
- Zero non-canonical signage tolerated

**Section 8 — Safety & Rules System**
- Safety is shared responsibility, not enforcement
- Staff authority is non-negotiable: if staff ask guests to adjust behavior, guests comply immediately; staff do not argue or negotiate
- Child supervision: children must be supervised at all times
- Decision rule: "If safety requires explanation or debate, the system has failed."

**Section 9 — TV Content System**
- Every TV has exactly one job — if it cannot be assigned one, it is turned off
- Three allowed roles: (1) Instruction Loop (30–60 sec — interaction, safety, feeding), (2) Education Loop (animal facts, conservation), (3) Ambient Loop (nature footage, atmosphere, no instructions or prices)
- Decision rule: "If a TV creates questions instead of answering them, it is wrong."

**Section 10 — Credits & Commercial Clarity**
- Core truth: guests do not need to spend extra money to enjoy the park
- Commercial communication must be calm, factual, presented once — no persuasion language
- Commercial signage appears only in designated locations; prices listed plainly; no animals on price signs; no safety language on price signs
- Donations are optional and never framed as obligation
- Decision rule: "If payment requires explanation, the system is wrong."

**Section 11 — Email & Phone System**
- Email and phone do not resolve issues — they acknowledge, route, and set expectations
- Email: automatic response only; no real-time staff monitoring
- Phone: voicemail/bot only; directs to booking or on-site assistance
- Decision rule: "If a guest needs email/phone to solve something, the system upstream is missing structure."

**Section 12 — Staff Expectations & Visibility**
- If clocked in, staff are visible, guest-facing, and engaged unless on assigned task
- Default position: on the floor
- Back rooms: only for assigned prep, animal care, stocking, short tasks — never hiding
- Guest engagement: acknowledge, smile, offer help. "Passing a guest without acknowledgment is a failure of the role."
- Clean as you go; reset your space

**Section 13 — Current Status & Lock State**
- Systems in this document are locked unless reality breaks them
- Paused by design (not revisited until physical order is restored): website build-out, graphic design execution, vinyl printing/installation, TV media production, automation tooling, future park rollout
- Decision rule: "If someone asks 'Are we still doing it this way?' the answer is 'Yes — it's locked.'"

**Section 14 — Execution & Replication**
- Execution order: (1) Language first → (2) Graphics second → (3) Physical installation third → (4) Staff orientation last
- Replication rule: nothing is customized per park; new needs are added to the canonical system and rolled out everywhere
- Decision rule: "If a solution only works because a specific person knows it, it is not complete."

**Section 15 — Direction Forward**
- Core posture: "We design systems once, then operate them."
- Change only when: reality contradicts the system, animal welfare is impacted, guest experience degrades, or staff cannot execute cleanly
- Final declaration: "Clarity replaces explanation. Structure replaces chaos. Generosity operates inside systems. The organization operates with quiet strength."

### Key Content

**1. LIFE Philosophy**
> "LIFE is an observation-based learning system. It teaches: perception, signal interpretation, system behavior, environmental influence. Core truth: What a system can sense shapes its reality. Environment shapes design."

**2. Boundary & Governance**
> "LIFE evaluates. LIFE does not decide. Operational authority remains separate. No philosophical system overrides: feeding, veterinary care, compliance, staffing."

**3. Educational Spine**
All learning follows this sequence: Perception → Signal → Response → Learning → Reproduction → Constraint → Environment → Cross-domain. Animals are case studies of systems.

**4. Operational Spine**
Strict separation of four lanes: Regulatory / Operational / Educational / Commercial. No blending.

**5. Totem System**
- **Master Totem (Internal):** Every species must include: Perception, Environment, Signal, Response, Learning, Reproduction, Constraint, Cross-domain. Plus identity: name, habitat, climate.
- **Student Totem (Public):** Eight simplified questions: What can it sense? Where does it live? How does it move? How does it learn? What are its limits? Final: "Think About This."

**6. Narrative Suites (20 — Locked)**
These are system concepts, not characters. Full list:
1. Reflection Suite — Perception ≠ reality
2. Perception Responsibility Suite — Ignoring signals causes failure
3. Illusion Suite — Signal ≠ substance
4. Signal Suite — Survival depends on detection
5. Signal Reliability Suite — False signals break systems
6. Naming Suite — Knowledge begins with naming
7. Systems Suite — Roles create stability
8. Cooperation Suite — Collective strength
9. Pattern Suite — Optimal ranges
10. Constraint Suite — Limits shape behavior
11. Resource Suite — Risk vs reward
12. Energy Economy Suite — Efficiency vs speed
13. Adaptation Suite — Environment shapes identity
14. Vertical Perspective Suite — Height changes awareness
15. Scale Suite — Size changes meaning
16. Transformation Suite — Appearance ≠ function
17. Fragility Suite — Environment mismatch
18. Resilience Suite — Structure under stress
19. Extreme Climate Suite — Specialization for survival
20. LIFE Synthesis Suite — All systems connect

**7. Primary Energy System (Plants)**
Plants are primary producers. Sun → Plant → Animal → System. Animals cannot survive without plants. Plants define energy entry into all systems.

**8. Diet & Encounter Control System (Locked)**
- Central Rule: All animal feeding is centralized. No independent feeding.
- Animal Diet Kitchen: all diets prepared there; only designated feeders allowed
- Diet Team: 3 designated feeders per park; they alone prepare, portion, and label bowls
- Bowl System: each bowl labeled with animal name, diet, date
- Encounter Allocation (Critical Fix): each species has Base Diet + Max Encounter Feed; when encounter feed is gone, encounters stop — no exceptions
- Escalation Rule: if diet seems wrong, report; do not change
- Absolute Rule: No new diet documents, no new folders, no improvisation

**9. Social Media Alignment**
Content must: teach biology, show supervised interaction, avoid exaggeration, avoid unsafe practices. Post structure: Hook → Explanation → Environment link → Reflection question.

**10. Implementation Rules**
- No redesigning systems
- No creating templates
- No adding documents
- Follow structure exactly

**Final Statement:** "This system is not optional. It is institutional. Consistency builds credibility. Structure prevents chaos."

## `Owners-Top-Ten-Canonical-1.pdf`

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **File** | `Owners-Top-Ten-Canonical-1.pdf` |
| **Pages** | 4 pages |
| **Document Type** | Owner's Standards / Non-Negotiable Operating Rules |
| **Relationship to LIFE** | Translates LIFE philosophy into the owner's direct, uncompromising operational expectations |

### Purpose
A canonical "owner's voice" document defining the ten non-negotiable observable standards for park operations. Exists in two versions: (1) Expanded canonical — full explanation; (2) Reduced door/back-of-house version — single-sentence rules for quick reference.

### Full Verbatim Text — Expanded Version

**1. Cleanliness Is Absolute**
Every space — public or non-public — must be clean at all times. If you see trash, you pick it up. No exceptions. Floors, glass, rails, exhibits, restrooms. Back rooms, prep rooms, offices, storage. No visible trash, food debris, wrappers, or clutter. "Someone else will get it" is not acceptable. Clean is the default state.

**2. Animals Are Fed for Engagement, Not Convenience**
Animals are not fed out in the morning to "get it done." Feeding is part of guest engagement. Animals must be motivated to interact. Staff feeding replaces guest interaction = failure. Prep food ≠ deliver food automatically. If animals are full before guests arrive, the experience is broken.

**3. Guest Interaction Is a Performance Requirement**
This is a public-facing environment. Smiles are required. Engagement is required. Ignoring guests is unacceptable. Passing a guest without acknowledgment is a failure. If you don't want to interact with people, this is not the right role.

**4. Most Encounters Are Free — Feeding Is Optional**
Guests may interact freely with most animals. Feeding costs money because food, prep, and staff time cost money. Touching, observing, and interacting are largely free. Staff may comp feeding at their discretion. Compassion and judgment are encouraged. We sell joy, not transactions.

**5. Species Rules Are Followed Exactly**
Every exhibit has specific interaction and feeding rules. No improvisation. No substitutions. No "I thought." SOPs override habit and memory. If you don't know, you ask.

**6. Staff Are Sensors, Not Decision Centers**
Staff observe and report. Management decides. Photos > opinions. Facts > interpretations. Report issues, don't redesign systems. Managers escalate, not negotiate reality. The brain decides. Sensors detect.

**7. Education Is Always On**
This park exists to educate — especially children. TVs must show approved content. Education must be simple and accurate. No misinformation. No random media. If a screen is off or wrong, it is fixed immediately.

**8. Accountability for Animal Welfare**
Every injury, illness, or death is documented. Cause is identified. SOP compliance is reviewed. Neglect has consequences. Care is a responsibility, not a suggestion.

**9. Order Prevents Drift**
Nothing is "temporary" forever. No taped signs. No handwritten notes. No drilling into acrylic. No mixed styles. If it doesn't match the standard, it gets removed.

**10. Continuous Improvement Is Required**
Learning never stops. Weekly reading or training. Short reflection required. Questions are encouraged. Drift is not tolerated. We improve intentionally or decay automatically.

### Reduced Version (Door/Back-of-House)
1. Cleanliness is constant. If you see trash, pick it up.
2. Animals are fed for guest engagement. Do not feed them out.
3. Guest interaction is required. Smiles and engagement are part of the job.
4. Most encounters are free. Feeding costs money; joy does not.
5. Follow species rules exactly. No guessing. No shortcuts.
6. Staff observe. Managers decide. Photos over opinions.
7. Education is always on. Screens must be correct or off.
8. Animal welfare is documented. Neglect has consequences.
9. No clutter. No temporary fixes. Remove what doesn't belong.
10. Improve or drift. Learning is mandatory.

## `PARK-WIDE-SIGNAGE-REPLACEMENT-MAP-1.pdf`

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **File** | `PARK-WIDE-SIGNAGE-REPLACEMENT-MAP-1.pdf` |
| **Pages** | 8 pages |
| **Document Type** | Visual Governance / Execution Plan |
| **Relationship to LIFE** | Implements the LIFE visual communication architecture across all physical spaces |

### Purpose
A canonical, execution-ready map governing what signage gets removed, what replaces it, where each type goes, and what the TV/screen system is reset to. Creates a park-wide visual governor so no one can "just print a sign" anymore.

### Global Removal Rule
Any sign that is: taped, laminated and curling, handwritten, mixed font/color, screwed or drilled into acrylic, contradicts canonical rules, "temporary but permanent," QR-only without context, says "childcare" when it's a paid experience, or explains pricing inconsistently — is removed. No exceptions. Subtraction comes before replacement.

### Five Permitted Sign Categories

**1. Species / Encounter Signs**
- Format: Below-Text Vertical Totem (Locked)
- Species covered: Ring-tailed lemur, Red ruffed lemur, Black & white ruffed lemur, Giraffe, Birds, Sloth
- Remove all current species signs; Replace with: image-only vertical graphic (top) + vinyl text box (bottom), acrylic mounted (no drilling)

**2. Instructional Signs (How To Interact)**
- Examples: "How to feed stingrays," "Where to stand," "Hands flat / two fingers / wait for animal"
- Rules: Icons + short commands only; no paragraphs; no biology facts here
- Action: Remove long instructional posters; Replace with icon-based vinyl at eye level near interaction point

**3. Educational / Conservation Signs**
- Examples: Overfishing, Climate/emissions, Animal facts, Impact reports
- Rules: Not encounter signs; go on education walls not inside exhibits; side-text panel format allowed
- Action: Group into zones; remove scattered education posters; reinstall in clean clusters

**4. Commercial / Pricing Signs**
- Must match staff packet exactly; no ambiguous words; one price = one meaning
- Action: Remove all old pricing signs; Replace with single standardized pricing vinyl at point of sale or decision only

**5. Directional / Wayfinding Signs**
- Icons + arrows only; no photos, no pricing, no text blocks
- Action: Remove mixed-style direction signs; Replace with icon-only vinyl or acrylic arrows

### TV Screen Hard Reset
Every screen must be assigned exactly ONE role:
- **A. Instruction:** feeding steps, interaction rules, safety loops
- **B. Education:** conservation, biology, impact
- **C. Ambient:** slow nature footage, calming visuals

Action: Turn off any screen that mixes roles; label internally; content comes later — role comes first.

### What Is Intentionally NOT Being Replaced Yet
- Reference photos (staff will generate)
- Biology / university signage
- Advanced AI visuals
- Projection mapping

### Execution Order (Required Sequence)
1. Remove all non-canonical signs
2. Lock roles (species / instruction / education / commercial / direction)
3. Install species totems first (starting with lemurs)
4. Add instructional vinyl at interactions
5. Consolidate education walls
6. Re-add pricing last
