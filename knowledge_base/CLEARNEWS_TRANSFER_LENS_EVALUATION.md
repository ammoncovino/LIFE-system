## Evaluation Protocol

Each section is assessed across 6 dimensions:

1. **STRUCTURE BEFORE BELIEF** — Are premises identified? Do conclusions follow from them?
2. **SEE (Input Discipline)** — What is directly observed vs. interpreted, assumed, or hearsay?
3. **HEAR (Mechanism Discipline)** — What causal mechanism is proposed? Is it testable?
4. **SPEAK (Output Discipline)** — Does the conclusion follow strictly, or does it overreach?
5. **FALSIFIABILITY** — Can the claims be proven wrong with evidence?
6. **OPERATIONAL MEANING** — Are there concrete, testable implications?

**Scoring (0.0–1.0):**
- `logic` — higher = better structural reasoning
- `rhetoric` — higher = worse (persuasion substituting for logic)
- `compression` — higher = worse (detail loss that changes meaning)
- `emotional_appeal` — higher = worse (affect substituting for evidence)
- `falsifiability` — higher = better (claims are testable)
- `operational_meaning` — higher = better (concrete, actionable implications)

### Rhetoric Markers (persuasion over logic):

- **"This is no longer a concept. It is a deployable system."** — This is the single most consequential claim in the document. A deployable system requires: running code, tested endpoints, hosted infrastructure, authenticated ingestion pipelines. If what exists is a system architecture document and a series of ChatGPT prompts, the correct structural term is "specification" or "design," not "deployable system." The sentence performs an undeclared escalation from Alpha (descriptive) to Omega (operational) — exactly what the author's own governance system is designed to prevent.

- **"Production-ready architecture"** (Section F) — "Production-ready" has a specific technical meaning: code that has been tested, handles edge cases, fails gracefully, and can serve real users under load. A ChatGPT conversation that describes rate limiting, caching, and auto-ingestion is not production-ready architecture. It is a design specification. The distinction matters because one requires investment to deploy and the other requires investment to build, test, deploy, and maintain. These are different by orders of magnitude.

- **"Institution-ready infrastructure"** (Section E) — Same pattern. Documentation that exists as ChatGPT output is a draft, not infrastructure. Infrastructure implies persistence, version control, review, and institutional adoption. The documentation stack may be high quality — but calling it "infrastructure" compresses the distance between "exists in a thread" and "exists in a system."

- **"You solved the hardest problem"** (Section D) — Unsubstantiated superlative. Which problem space? Harder than what? By whose measure? The language translation work described (converting technical structure to human-readable language without moralizing) is genuinely valuable and genuinely rare. But "solved" implies completion, and "hardest" implies a comparison that hasn't been established. Structural correction: "You demonstrated a working approach to the hardest problem."

- **"👉 This is your primary interpretive engine"** — The arrow-and-bold format performs authority. It tells the reader what to believe about the item's importance rather than letting the reader determine importance from evidence. This is a transmission pattern, not a structural one.

### Logic Markers (structural reasoning demonstrated):

- The six-part taxonomy (Core System, Diagnostics Tool, Product System, Translation Layer, Documentation Stack, API/Backend) is a genuine structural differentiation. These are distinct functional categories, and organizing them this way is sound.
- The identification of the translation layer as a separate, valuable component is a legitimate structural insight — most analysis systems fail at this step.

### Alpha-Omega Gate Analysis:

**Gate A Violation (Alpha → Prescriptive without consent):**  
The document presents itself as descriptive ("what was built") but is actually prescriptive ("here is what you should believe about what was built"). No assumptions are declared. No alternatives are presented. The framing assumes the reader agrees these are "real assets" before presenting evidence.

**Gate B Violation (Prescriptive → Omega without boundary):**  
"Deployable system" and "production-ready" are operational claims. They imply the next step is deployment. This skips scope boundary, reversibility awareness, and accountability ownership. Who deploys? With what resources? Based on what testing?

**Drift Classification: HIGH**
