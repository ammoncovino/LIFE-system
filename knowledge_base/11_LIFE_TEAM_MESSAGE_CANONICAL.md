Form — physical structure and constraint

Political instruction

## [2/7, 7:17 PM] Ammon Covino: AI AS INTERPRETIVE LAYER — LIFE


Status: PHILOSOPHY ONLY · NON-EXECUTING Applies to: LIFE — Language, Intelligence, Form, Ecology Purpose: Define how AI may be used to support learning without replacing agency, observation, or human judgment.


---


### CORE STATEMENT


In LIFE, AI is not a teacher, authority, or guide.


AI functions as an interpretive layer — a tool that helps learners translate observation into understanding without telling them what to believe.


The learner remains primary. The system remains observable. The AI remains constrained.


---


### WHY AN INTERPRETIVE LAYER


Fixed explanations (screens, scripts, lectures) collapse learning into a single narrative.


An interpretive layer allows:


Inquiry to begin with observation


Depth to scale with curiosity


Context to travel with the learner


This preserves agency and reduces noise.


---


### WHAT AI IS ALLOWED TO DO


AI may:


Describe what is observable


Explain mechanisms and constraints


Compare systems without ranking them


Translate unfamiliar phenomena into accessible language


Answer questions generated by the learner


AI may respond to:


Images taken by the learner


Natural language questions


Requests for clarification


---


### WHAT AI IS NOT ALLOWED TO DO


AI must not:


Persuade or convince


Moralize or assign value judgments


Replace human educators or staff


Direct behavior beyond safety boundaries


Infer intent, motivation, or belief


Present speculation as fact


AI does not decide meaning. AI does not resolve disagreement.


---


### OBSERVATION FIRST RULE


In LIFE, learning begins with seeing.


AI is invoked only after observation, not before.


This preserves the primacy of:


the organism


the environment


the system itself


---


### PERSONALIZED DEPTH WITHOUT FRAGMENTATION


Because the interpretive layer travels with the learner:


Children may ask simple questions


Adults may explore deeper structure


Context can accumulate across spaces


No two learning paths must be identical.


---


### NO REQUIRED TECHNOLOGY


LIFE does not require proprietary software, screens, or platforms.


Any large language model may be used. Use is encouraged, not mandatory.


Learning remains accessible without AI.


---


### ETHICAL GOVERNOR


AI use in LIFE is constrained by the Reality-First Governor:


Mechanism before narrative


Limits before conclusions


Observation before interpretation


If an AI output conflicts with observable reality, the observation prevails.


---


### ROLE OF STAFF


Staff:


Encourage observation


Support questions


Maintain safety


Staff do not:


Interpret on behalf of learners


Paraphrase AI responses as authority


Use AI to control outcomes


---


### FINAL PRINCIPLE


AI extends the learner’s ability to ask better questions.


It does not replace curiosity. It does not replace experience. It does not replace responsibility.
