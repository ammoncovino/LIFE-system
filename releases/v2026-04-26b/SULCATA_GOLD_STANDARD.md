# SULCATA TORTOISE — GOLD STANDARD DIET (LOCKED)

**Captured from owner directive, 2026-04-26.**

Native range: Sahel and southern Sahara — semi-arid grasslands, savanna, thorn shrubland.

## Diet boundary (the rule)

**The diet is what they would naturally come across in their wild range, by percentage of natural diet. Nothing else.**

---

## ~90% — Grasses and hay (the base)

This is what's in the bowl. Whole, dry, fragrant.

- Grass hay (timothy, orchard, Bermuda, meadow grass)
- Fresh grass when available

**No alfalfa hay** — too high in protein and oxalates for grazers.

## ~10% — Wild-range supplements (enrichment, treats)

Things they encounter in their natural habitat. Staff and guests may offer these as treats — not in the feeding bowl.

- **Dandelions** (greens and flowers)
- **Prickly pear cactus** — pads and flowers (opuntia)
- **Hibiscus** flowers and leaves
- **Pumpkin and melon** — sometimes husk and all
- **Other desert/savanna flowers and weeds** they would find in the wild

---

## Operational rule

- Bowl = hay only. Always visible.
- Enrichment = items above, hand-fed or scattered, never replacing hay.
- Baseline hay before guest hours; do not feed to fullness before guests arrive.
- All items must be pesticide- and herbicide-free.

## Boundary

If the item is in their wild range, it's allowed. If it's not in their wild range, it's not on the card. No kale, romaine, carrots, apples — those are not part of the rule.
