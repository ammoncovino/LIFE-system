## Why this matters

The previous canonical card set treated tortoise care as a single concept (sulcata-like grazing). In reality, the four tortoise species in our facilities have **fundamentally different wild diets**:

| Species | Wild diet rule |
|---|---|
| **Sulcata** | Hay-only grazer (the ONLY hay-only species in the canon) |
| **Leopard** | Savanna grazer — grasses + greens, NO fruit, NO protein |
| **Russian** | Dry-steppe herbivore — leafy greens dominate, NO protein, fruit very rare |
| **Red-foot** | Forest-edge omnivore — fruit-dominant, weekly protein |
| **Yellow-foot** | Deep-rainforest omnivore — greens-dominant, weekly protein (OPPOSITE of red-foot) |

Treating red-foot and yellow-foot as the same diet (the issue Ammon flagged) produces digestive imbalance — the wild science is clear that they have inverse macronutrient profiles.
