SLUG: toco-toucan
NAME: TOCO TOUCAN
SCI: Ramphastos toco
WHERE: South America — forest edges, open woodland, and savanna
CONS: Least concern

Q1 sense:
  - Its long bill reaches fruit at branch tips too thin to climb.
  - Its eyes give it good color vision for spotting ripe fruit.
  - Its hearing is sharp and it listens for calls from other toucans.
  - Its bill-clacking makes a sound that communicates with nearby birds.
Q2 habitat:
  - It lives in forests, forest edges, and open woodland across South America.
  - It needs trees with hollow cavities to nest in.
  - Fruiting trees in its home range must produce fruit through different seasons.
  - It also visits savanna areas where scattered trees hold fruit.
Q3 signals:
  - It produces a deep, repeated croaking call that carries through the forest.
  - Bill-clacking communicates with a mate or a nearby rival.
  - It deposits seeds inside the nest cavity before laying eggs.
  - Body posture and bill angle signal aggression or calm to nearby birds.
Q4 change:
  - It flies in a quick, undulating path when alarmed.
  - It stays on high perches during disturbance, watching from above.
  - It avoids fruit with high iron content, which can harm its health.
  - In open habitats it flies between trees rather than staying in the canopy.
Q5 learn:
  - Nestlings learn to beg loudly — their calls prompt both parents to bring food.
  - Fledglings follow parents to fruiting trees before foraging on their own.
  - Young toucans learn through observation which fruit sources to visit and when.
Q6 babies:
  - A pair lays two to four eggs in a hollow tree cavity.
  - Both the male and the female take turns incubating the eggs.
  - Both parents feed and brood the nestlings until they can fly.
  - Chicks are blind and naked at hatching and grow slowly inside the cavity.
Q7 limits:
  - It needs existing hollow tree cavities — it cannot excavate its own nest.
  - Loss of large old trees removes the nesting sites the species depends on.
  - Its fruit diet must stay low in iron, limiting which fruits it eats.
  - Loud disturbance near nest cavities can cause parents to abandon the nest.
Q8 teaches:
  - Its large bill reaches food, signals to other birds, and helps regulate body heat.
  - Both parents sharing nest duties increases the survival rate of young.
  - Toucans swallow and drop seeds far from the parent tree, helping the forest spread.
