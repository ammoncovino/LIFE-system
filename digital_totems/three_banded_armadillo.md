# THREE-BANDED ARMADILLO
*Tolypeutes matacus*

Order: Cingulata
Native to: Central South America (Gran Chaco region)
Diet: Omnivore (insects, larvae, fruit, plant matter)
Respiration: Lungs

1. **What does it see?**
Moderate vision detects movement across ground habitat.
Visual cues assist navigation between burrows and foraging areas.

2. **What does it sense?**
Highly developed smell detects insects and buried food sources.
Sensitive hearing detects approaching predators or ground disturbance.

3. **How does it move?**
Walks using strong forelimbs and digging claws.
When threatened, rolls body into a complete armored sphere.

4. **How does it breathe?**
Air enters lungs through nasal passages and trachea.
Alveoli exchange oxygen supporting ground foraging activity.

5. **What fuels it?**
Insects, larvae, fruit, and plant material provide metabolic energy.
Invertebrates supply protein and fats.

6. **What limits it?**
Dependent on warm climates and accessible soil for foraging.
Extended cold or habitat disturbance reduces survival.

7. **What decision is constant?**
Continue digging for food or move to new ground?
Remain exposed or roll into protective armor?

8. **What role does it play?**
Consumes insects that influence soil ecosystems.
Disturbs soil while digging, affecting nutrient cycling.
