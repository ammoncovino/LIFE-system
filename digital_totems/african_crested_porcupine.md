# AFRICAN CRESTED PORCUPINE
*Hystrix cristata*

Order: Rodentia
Native to: North Africa and parts of sub-Saharan Africa
Diet: Herbivore (roots, tubers, bark, fruit)
Respiration: Lungs

1. **What does it see?**
Moderate vision suited for low-light activity.
Detects movement and obstacles while moving at night.

2. **What does it sense?**
Strong smell identifies food sources and territorial markings.
Sensitive hearing detects approaching animals in darkness.

3. **How does it move?**
Walks with sturdy limbs and strong digging claws.
Raises quills and rattles hollow tail quills when threatened.

4. **How does it breathe?**
Air enters lungs through nasal passages and trachea.
Oxygen exchange in alveoli supports slow, steady movement.

5. **What fuels it?**
Roots, tubers, bark, and fallen fruit provide plant energy.
Occasional bone gnawing supplies minerals such as calcium.

6. **What limits it?**
Dependent on access to vegetation and burrow shelter.
Extended drought or habitat disturbance reduces food availability.

7. **What decision is constant?**
Continue feeding or retreat to burrow?
Stand ground with quills raised or withdraw?

8. **What role does it play?**
Consumes roots and vegetation, influencing plant distribution.
Burrowing disturbs soil and creates shelter used by other animals.

How does an animal survive at night when vision is limited?
