# PATAGONIAN CAVY
*Dolichotis patagonum*

Order: Rodentia
Native to: Argentina (Patagonian steppe)
Diet: Herbivore (grasses and shrubs)
Respiration: Lungs

1. **What does it see?**
Eyes positioned laterally provide wide field-of-view across open steppe.
Vision detects movement across grassland and distant terrain.

2. **What does it sense?**
Sensitive hearing detects approaching predators across open ground.
Olfactory signals identify territory and social partners.

3. **How does it move?**
Walks and grazes using quadrupedal movement.
Escapes predators with rapid running and bounding strides.

4. **How does it breathe?**
Air enters lungs through nasal passages and trachea.
Alveoli exchange oxygen to sustain running and grazing activity.

5. **What fuels it?**
Grasses and low vegetation processed through hindgut fermentation.
Microbial digestion converts plant fiber into usable energy.

6. **What limits it?**
Dependent on open grassland habitat and seasonal plant growth.
Habitat degradation or drought reduces forage availability.

7. **What decision is constant?**
Continue grazing or scan for predators?
Remain near burrow shelter or move to new forage?

8. **What role does it play?**
Grazing herbivore shaping grassland vegetation.
Prey species supporting predator populations in Patagonian ecosystems.

How does living in open landscapes change how an animal detects danger?
