# BROWN LEMUR
*Eulemur fulvus*

Order: Primates
Native to: Madagascar
Diet: Omnivore (fruit-dominant)
Respiration: Lungs

1. **What does it see?**
Forward-facing eyes detect color and depth in forest canopy.
Visual tracking supports fruit selection and branch landing.

2. **What does it sense?**
Strong olfactory detection identifies scent marks and reproductive signals.
Vocal calls coordinate troop spacing and alert responses.

3. **How does it move?**
Quadrupedal movement along branches and ground travel when needed.
Hind limbs support short leaps between trees.

4. **How does it breathe?**
Air enters lungs through nasal passages and trachea.
Alveoli exchange oxygen to sustain active foraging.

5. **What fuels it?**
Fruit sugars provide primary metabolic energy.
Leaves, flowers, and small invertebrates supplement nutrients.

6. **What limits it?**
Dependent on seasonal fruit availability and forest structure.
Habitat fragmentation reduces food access and travel routes.

7. **What decision is constant?**
Continue feeding or move to a new food source?
Remain with troop or shift position within the group?

8. **What role does it play?**
Disperses seeds across forest ecosystems.
Supports regeneration of fruiting tree species.

**Reflection**
How would a forest change if animals that move seeds disappeared?
