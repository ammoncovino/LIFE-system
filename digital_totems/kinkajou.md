# KINKAJOU
*Potos flavus*

Order: Carnivora
Native to: Central and South America
Diet: Omnivore (fruit-dominant)
Respiration: Lungs

1. **What does it see?**
Large forward-facing eyes adapted for low-light forest canopy conditions.
Vision detects fruit, branches, and movement at night.

2. **What does it sense?**
Strong sense of smell locates ripe fruit and nectar sources.
Sensitive hearing detects canopy movement and nearby animals.

3. **How does it move?**
Arboreal climbing using grasping hands and feet.
Prehensile tail stabilizes body while moving along branches.

4. **How does it breathe?**
Air enters lungs through nasal passages and trachea.
Oxygen exchange occurs in alveoli supporting nocturnal activity.

5. **What fuels it?**
Fruit sugars provide primary metabolic energy.
Nectar, insects, and small vertebrates supplement nutrients.

6. **What limits it?**
Dependent on continuous tropical forest canopy and fruit availability.
Habitat fragmentation reduces travel routes and feeding access.

7. **What decision is constant?**
Continue feeding in current tree or travel to new fruit source?
Remain hidden or shift position in canopy cover?

8. **What role does it play?**
Consumes fruit and disperses seeds in tropical forests.
Pollinates flowering plants while feeding on nectar.

How does moving through tree canopies change how an animal finds food?
