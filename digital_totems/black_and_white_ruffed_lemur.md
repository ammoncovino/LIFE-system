# BLACK-AND-WHITE RUFFED LEMUR
*Varecia variegata*

Order: Primates
Native to: Eastern Madagascar
Diet: Omnivore (fruit-dominant)
Respiration: Lungs

1. **What does it see?**
Forward-facing eyes detect color and depth in dense forest canopy.
Visual tracking supports branch navigation and fruit selection.

2. **What does it sense?**
Highly developed smell detects scent marks and reproductive signals.
Loud vocal calls coordinate spacing between individuals in forest canopy.

3. **How does it move?**
Arboreal quadrupedal movement across branches.
Powerful hind limbs enable long leaps between trees.

4. **How does it breathe?**
Air-breathing mammal using lungs.
Oxygen exchange occurs in alveoli to support sustained movement.

5. **What fuels it?**
Primarily fruit sugars converted to metabolic energy.
Leaves, nectar, and occasional invertebrates supplement nutrients.

6. **What limits it?**
Dependent on intact rainforest canopy and seasonal fruit cycles.
Forest fragmentation restricts travel and food access.

7. **What decision is constant?**
Continue feeding or relocate to a new fruit source?
Remain with vocal group contact or shift position in the canopy?

8. **What role does it play?**
Major seed disperser for many rainforest tree species.
Supports regeneration and diversity of Madagascar forests.

**Reflection**
What happens to a rainforest if animals that spread seeds disappear?
