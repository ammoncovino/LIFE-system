# TWO-TOED SLOTH
*Choloepus didactylus*

Order: Pilosa
Native to: Northern South America
Diet: Herbivore (leaves, shoots, fruit)
Respiration: Lungs

1. **What does it see?**
Vision adapted for low-light forest canopy conditions.
Detects nearby branches and movement while suspended.

2. **What does it sense?**
Sensitive smell detects leaves and nearby environmental cues.
Hearing detects canopy disturbances or approaching animals.

3. **How does it move?**
Suspends body from branches using long curved claws.
Moves slowly through canopy using forelimb pulling motion.

4. **How does it breathe?**
Air enters lungs through nasal passages and trachea.
Oxygen exchange occurs in alveoli during slow metabolic activity.

5. **What fuels it?**
Leaves digested slowly through microbial fermentation in multi-chamber stomach.
Low-energy plant material supports slow metabolism.

6. **What limits it?**
Dependent on continuous tree canopy and specific leaf sources.
Cold temperatures and habitat loss reduce survival.

7. **What decision is constant?**
Remain resting or move to new feeding branch?
Stay concealed or shift position in canopy?

8. **What role does it play?**
Consumes leaves and influences tree growth patterns.
Provides habitat for algae and small organisms within its fur ecosystem.

**Reflection**
How does a slow metabolism change how an animal survives in the forest?
