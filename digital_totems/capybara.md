# CAPYBARA
*Hydrochoerus hydrochaeris*

Order: Rodentia
Native to: South America
Diet: Herbivore (grasses and aquatic plants)
Respiration: Lungs

1. **What does it see?**
Eyes positioned high on the head scan while the body remains submerged.
Vision detects movement along water edges and open grasslands.

2. **What does it sense?**
Sensitive hearing detects approaching movement or predators.
Scent glands communicate territory and group identity.

3. **How does it move?**
Walks and runs on land using sturdy limbs.
Swims efficiently with partially webbed feet.

4. **How does it breathe?**
Air-breathing mammal using lungs.
Nostrils close slightly while swimming and surface breathing is frequent.

5. **What fuels it?**
Grasses and aquatic vegetation converted through hindgut fermentation.
Microbial digestion breaks down plant fiber into usable energy.

6. **What limits it?**
Requires constant access to freshwater for cooling and escape.
Extended drought or loss of wetland habitat reduces survival.

7. **What decision is constant?**
Remain grazing or retreat to water?
Stay with group protection or move to new forage?

8. **What role does it play?**
Large herbivore shaping grass and wetland vegetation.
Provides prey biomass for major predators in its ecosystem.

**Reflection**
How does access to water change how an animal survives?
