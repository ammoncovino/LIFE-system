# GOLD STANDARD DIET RULE — LOCKED

**Captured from owner directive, 2026-04-26.**

## The rule (verbatim intent)

> The gold standard diet for all animals is the things they would naturally come across in the wild, based on percentages of their natural diet. Nothing else.

## How it applies operationally

**Base feeding:** the natural-wild items that make up the bulk of the species' real diet, in their natural proportions.

**Enrichment (treats, not the diet):** other items the species would find in their natural environment that staff or guests can offer for enrichment. These are NOT the diet — they are extras within the wild-diet boundary.

## What this rule excludes

- **No invented "WRONG food" lists** of items the animal would never encounter in the wild (kale, romaine, apple, carrot, bread, cake, etc.). If it's not in the wild diet, it's simply not on the card. Listing it makes the card longer and signals the operator doesn't know the animal.
- **No AZA/zoo-industry standards.** The rule is the species' wild diet, not what other facilities do.
- **No web-sourced ingredient lists.** Source is the owner's directives and the animal's natural ecology.

## Sulcata Tortoise — applied

**Wild diet:** African grassland/savanna grazer. Eats grasses, dry forage, and seasonal vegetation.

**Base feeding:** hay, and only hay (grass hay or timothy — both are dry grass). This covers the "graze on dry grass" reality of where the animal lives.

**Enrichment within wild diet:** flowers (they love flowers — it reads as a treat, and flowers are something they'd genuinely encounter in their range), other wild-range plants like dandelion or prickly pear pads when seasonally appropriate, browse the animal would actually come across.

**NOT on the card:** kale, romaine, carrots, apples, etc. — none of these exist where a Sulcata lives, so listing them wastes space and signals ignorance.

## Why this format wins

- **Shorter** — every line is a real wild-diet item; no padding.
- **Defensible** — anyone with knowledge of the species reading it can verify it against the species' actual ecology.
- **Educational** — staff who feed the animal learn what the animal IS, not just what to avoid.
- **No drift** — the rule is biological, not preference-based. The animal's range is the boundary.

## Build process for posters

1. State the species and its wild range/biome.
2. List the natural-wild base diet (the bulk of what they really eat).
3. List the enrichment items — also from the wild range — that staff/guests can offer as treats.
4. State the operational rule for the facility (e.g., "hay only for base feeding; flowers as enrichment").
5. Locked footer: "Match the bowl. Don't interpret." + "What a living thing can sense becomes its reality."

That's it. No "WRONG" gallery. No invented prohibitions. The wild diet is the boundary.
