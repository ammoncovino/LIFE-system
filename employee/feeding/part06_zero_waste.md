# PART 6 — THE ZERO WASTE FOOD LOOP

---

## System Overview

**System Name:** Zero Waste Food Loop System
**System Type:** Closed-loop biological conversion system
**Core Principle:** Nothing is discarded. All organic material is converted into life through structured stages.

---

## The Loop

**Main Loop:** Food waste → Compost → Insect biomass → Chickens → Eggs → Animal feed
**Parallel Loop:** Barley → Sprouting → Chicken feed → Egg production support
**Exclusion:** The outside aviary is excluded from the collection process.

---

## The 7 Stages

### Stage 1 — Collection
Capture ALL uneaten food from designated exhibits into ONE central bin. Every exhibit has a collection protocol. Follow it.

### Stage 2 — Sorting
Separate collected material into three categories:
- **A) Primary Compost:** Fruits, vegetables, grains, plant material
- **B) Controlled:** Meat, eggs, protein
- **C) Reject / Escalate:** Plastic, trash, anything unknown — ASK if unsure

### Stage 3 — Compost
Break down organic material. Requires moisture balance, airflow, and warmth. Follow the composting protocol.

### Stage 4 — Insect Conversion
Black soldier fly larvae convert compost into protein biomass. This is a managed biological process.

### Stage 5 — Chicken Feeding
Insects from Stage 4 plus barley sprouts feed the chickens.

### Stage 6 — Egg Production
Chickens produce eggs — a high-density nutrient package.

### Stage 7 — Animal Feed
Eggs are returned to the broader animal feeding system.
