## SECTION 1 — OWNER'S TOP TEN EVALUATION

### Current Top Ten (from printed sign)

1. Cleanliness is constant. If you see trash, pick it up.
2. Animals are fed for guest engagement. Do not feed them out.
3. Guest interaction is required. Smiles and engagement are part of the job.
4. Most encounters are free. Feeding costs money; joy does not.
5. Follow species rules exactly. No guessing. No shortcuts.
6. Staff observe. Managers decide. Photos over opinions.
7. Education is always on. Screens must be correct or off.
8. Animal welfare is documented. Neglect has consequences.
9. No clutter. No temporary fixes. Remove what doesn't belong.
10. Improve or drift. Learning is mandatory.

### Problems Found

**A. The #1 Rule Is Missing From the Top Ten.**
"If it is not assigned, written, or approved — do not do it." This is the single most important operational rule in the entire system. It's the rule that prevents exactly what happened with the animal care software — staff improvisation, unauthorized redesigns, drifting systems. It appears in the Back Room Sign, the Orientation Walkthrough, and every SOP. But it's not Rule #1 on the Top Ten. That's a structural failure.

**B. Rule #1 (Cleanliness) is correct behavior but not a governing principle.**
"If you see trash, pick it up" is a task. It belongs on a daily standards list, not as the opening statement of your highest-authority document. It doesn't set the tone for "this is how authority works here."

**C. Rule #10 ("Improve or drift") is vague.**
What does "improve" mean to a 19-year-old who has been throwing away 15 pounds of food a day? Without specificity, this becomes motivational poster language. Staff will read it, nod, and do nothing differently.

**D. Rule #4 (Free vs Paid encounters) is operational detail, not a governing principle.**
This is important information for the floor. It's not a Top Ten commandment. It belongs in the Staff Quick Reference (where it already exists).

**E. Rule #6 ("Photos over opinions") is strong but misplaced.**
The concept — documentation beats verbal claims — is excellent. But it's buried at #6 behind cleanliness and smiling. It should be higher because it's a verification mechanism, not a preference.

**F. Food waste is not mentioned.**
You just described a $7.5M labor explosion driven partly by 60 bags of produce every couple of days, 15 lbs thrown away daily, and staff buying Missouri diet for every animal. The Zero Waste Food Loop is now a formal system. But the Top Ten says nothing about food waste. The list that's supposed to govern everything doesn't mention the problem that nearly sank the business.

**G. The Authorization Chain is absent.**
Who can approve changes? Who can sign contracts? Who can modify exhibits? The Top Ten doesn't address this. It's the gap that let staff redesign exhibits and let the animal care software guy order product without your knowledge.

### Recommendation: Not 5, Not 10 — Keep 10 But Rewrite Them

5 is too few. You'd have to compress so much that you lose the governing breadth.
20 is too many. Staff won't internalize 20 rules. The list becomes a manual, not a set of principles.
10 is the right number — but they need to be the RIGHT 10.

### Proposed New Top Ten

| # | New Rule | Why |
|---|---------|-----|
| 1 | **If it is not assigned, written, or approved — do not do it.** | THE rule. Prevents all unauthorized action. Should be first. |
| 2 | **Animal welfare overrides everything. No exceptions.** | Establishes animal care as the non-negotiable priority above convenience, speed, or habit. |
| 3 | **Follow the written diet. No substitutions. No guessing. Photo every feeding.** | Directly prevents the Missouri diet disaster. Combines the feeding rule with verification. |
| 4 | **Nothing gets thrown away. All food follows the Zero Waste loop.** | Addresses the 15-lbs-a-day waste problem directly. Makes the food loop a governing principle, not just an SOP. |
| 5 | **If you are not sure — STOP. ASK. WAIT.** | The uncertainty protocol. Prevents improvisation at the moment of doubt. |
| 6 | **Documentation is proof. No photo = not done. No record = did not happen.** | Elevates the photo verification system to a principle. "Photos over opinions" but sharper. |
| 7 | **Cleanliness is constant. No clutter. No temporary fixes.** | Merges old #1 and #9 into one. Clean is non-negotiable, but it's not the top priority — animal welfare and authorization are. |
| 8 | **Guest interaction is required. Be visible, engaged, and honest.** | Guest-facing behavior. Slightly reworded to include honesty — staff should not make up facts (ties to education rule). |
| 9 | **Education is always on. Do not create, edit, or replace system materials.** | Combines old #7 with the critical prohibition against staff-generated content drift. This is what let staff rewrite exhibits. |
| 10 | **Only authorized individuals sign contracts, approve purchases, or modify systems.** | The authority chain. Prevents the animal care software scenario — where someone brought in a vendor, ordered product, and restructured operations without owner approval. |

### What Changed and Why

- **Authorization Rule moved to #1** — It's the root cause of every problem you described.
- **Food waste and Zero Waste Loop now have their own rule (#4)** — This was a $4M+ problem that had no representation in the Top Ten.
- **Free vs Paid encounters removed** — Operational detail, already covered in Staff Quick Reference.
- **"Improve or drift" removed** — Too vague for a governing document. Learning expectations belong in the orientation, not the commandments.
- **Contract/purchase authority added (#10)** — Closes the gap that let unauthorized people bind you to vendors and products.
- **Cleanliness moved from #1 to #7** — Still mandatory, but not the governing philosophy of the business.

## SECTION 2 — FULL SYSTEM GAPS ANALYSIS

### GAP 1: No Inventory / Purchasing Control System

**Problem:** You described staff ordering 60 bags of produce every couple of days, buying 32 varieties of Missouri diet. The animal care software guy told them what to order.

**Current state in your system:** NOTHING. There is no purchasing SOP, no approved vendor list, no purchasing authority chain, no budget controls in any document.

**Fix needed:** A Purchasing Authority Protocol that defines:
- Who can order product (name/role, not department)
- Approved vendor list
- Budget thresholds that require owner approval
- Required documentation for all purchases
- No new vendors without owner sign-off

### GAP 2: No "What Animals Actually Eat" Master Diet Document

**Problem:** Your system has a Diet Verification Protocol (prepare, verify, photograph, deliver) and it's well-designed. But it verifies that the diet sheet was followed — it doesn't define what the diet sheet says.

**Current state:** The protocol assumes correct diet sheets exist. But the animal care software created WRONG diet sheets (Missouri diet for giraffes). If staff follow a bad diet sheet perfectly, your verification system will approve it.

**Fix needed:** A Master Diet Authority document (or declaration) that establishes:
- Diets are written by the owner or owner-designated authority ONLY
- No staff, software, or vendor can create or modify diet sheets
- The diet sheet itself must be signed/dated by the authority
- Any diet sheet without an authority signature is void

### GAP 3: No Technology / Software Governance

**Problem:** The animal care software is exactly how the business drifted. Someone introduced a tool, staff adopted it, and it became the de facto operating system — without your approval.

**Current state:** The AI Governance document (02_AI_GOVERNANCE.md) covers AI as an interpretive tool for visitors. It says nothing about operational software, task management tools, or external systems that staff might introduce.

**Fix needed:** A Technology Governance Rule:
- No software, app, or tracking system is adopted without owner written approval
- No external party configures operational systems
- If a vendor offers a "better way to organize" — the answer is NO until the owner evaluates it
- This applies to task trackers, scheduling apps, diet software, communication platforms, everything

### GAP 4: No Onboarding Sequence Beyond the Walkthrough

**Problem:** You have the Staff Orientation Walkthrough (10 pages, just built). You have the Staff Quick Reference. But there's no defined sequence for how a NEW hire goes from Day 1 to "operational."

**Current state:** The walkthrough is for existing staff during the reset. But what about the person you hire next month?

**Fix needed:** A New Hire Onboarding Sequence:
- Day 1: Orientation Walkthrough (face-to-face, signed)
- Day 1: Staff Quick Reference issued
- Day 1: Top Ten posted in their work area
- Week 1: Shadow period — observe only, no independent task execution
- Week 2: Supervised task execution — claim tasks, complete with verification
- Week 3: Independent execution — still photo-verified
- 30-Day Review: Manager confirms competency or extends probation

### GAP 5: Redundancy and Overlap Across Documents

**Problem:** The same concepts appear in multiple places with slightly different wording, which creates interpretation gaps.

**Examples:**
- The Authorization Rule appears in: Back Room Sign, Operations SOPs (x3 — Staff Control Rule, Authorization Model, Staff Control System Overview), Orientation Walkthrough, Staff Quick Reference, Master Export
- The Diet Verification Protocol appears in: Operations SOPs (x3 — Diet Verification Protocol, Diet Photo Example Guide, Diet Verification Model)
- The Escalation Ladder appears in: Operations SOPs (x2 — Escalation Ladder, Escalation Model), Staff Quick Reference, Orientation Walkthrough

**This is not necessarily bad** — repetition across staff-facing documents is intentional. The problem is that the KNOWLEDGE BASE has the same concept described 3 different ways, which could lead to inconsistency over time.

**Fix needed:** Designate ONE version as canonical for each core concept. Other documents reference it, not restate it. Example: "Diet Verification Protocol" in 03_Operations_SOPs.md is the canonical version. The Staff Quick Reference and Walkthrough summarize it but defer to the SOP.

### GAP 6: No Exhibit Modification Control

**Problem:** "They started changing the design of the exhibits."

**Current state:** The education system says staff can't edit education content. But there's no explicit rule about physical exhibit modifications — moving barriers, drilling, changing layouts, adding decorations.

**Fix needed:** An Exhibit Integrity Rule:
- No physical modification to any exhibit without owner written approval
- No drilling, painting, moving barriers, adding decorations, changing lighting
- "If it wasn't there yesterday and you didn't get written approval, remove it"

### GAP 7: No Waste Tracking / Measurement System

**Problem:** "When I started tracking the amount of food... they were throwing away about 15 pounds of food and produce."

**Current state:** The Zero Waste Food Loop system defines the process for converting waste. But there's no measurement system — no daily weigh-in, no waste log, no tracking against a target.

**Fix needed:** A Waste Tracking Protocol:
- Weigh all discarded material daily
- Log: date, weight, category (compost/controlled/reject)
- Target: trending toward zero reject weight
- Weekly review by manager
- Monthly review by owner

### GAP 8: The "Staff Message — Claim-Based Work System" Is Too Soft

**Problem:** The current staff message in the knowledge base says: "No one is required to act. No updates are required. No discussion is required. We will let the system show where clarity emerges."

This was written for a gradual introduction. You're now doing a FULL RESET. The tone needs to match.

**Fix needed:** A new staff message for the reset that says:
- We are starting over
- The old system is gone
- Here is the new system
- You will be walked through it in person
- You will sign acknowledging you understand
- Compliance is not optional

### GAP 9: No Labor Cost Control Mechanism

**Problem:** Labor went from $3.5M to $7.5-8M. The animal care software created busywork. Staff multiplied tasks.

**Current state:** The system defines how tasks are assigned (claim-based) but doesn't address task volume governance — who decides how many tasks exist, how they're reviewed for necessity, or how labor hours are tracked against output.

**Fix needed:** A Task Volume Governance concept:
- All recurring tasks must be reviewed and approved by owner/manager
- Busywork = any task that exists because "we've always done it" without verification that it serves animal welfare, guest experience, or facility integrity
- Quarterly task audit: review all recurring tasks against these three criteria
- If a task doesn't serve one of those three, it gets cut

### GAP 10: Species Totem System — Architecture Exists, Content Does Not

**Problem:** The Totem system is fully designed (8 micro-questions per species, structure locked). But only Ring-Tailed Lemur and Mudskipper are complete. Every other species is empty.

**Current state:** This is noted as an Open Thread. But in a full reset where you're reintroducing education, the totems are what the guests actually see.

**Priority:** This needs to be on the reset timeline. Not all species at once — but a first batch covering the anchor species that guests interact with most.

## SECTION 3 — WHAT'S STRONG (DO NOT CHANGE)

1. **The Authorization Rule.** "If it is not assigned, written, or approved — do not do it." This is the most important rule in the system and it's well-defined.

2. **Diet Verification Protocol.** Prepare → Verify → Photograph → Deliver. Clean, enforceable, measurable.

3. **LIFE Education Spine.** Deep, principled, mechanism-first. The distinction between structure and transmission is brilliant and unique.

4. **Zero Waste Food Loop.** The 7-stage closed-loop system is elegant and solves a real operational problem while creating educational value.

5. **Claim-Based Work System.** Posted → Claimed → Executed → Verified. Eliminates passive task failure. Simple enough that even resistant staff can understand it.

6. **Escalation Ladder.** 3-strike model is clear. Correction → Warning → Discipline. No ambiguity.

7. **Photo Verification as Universal Standard.** "No photo = not done" is enforceable across all systems.

8. **The Orientation Walkthrough.** One concept per page, visual-first, sign-off requirement. Designed for the reality that staff don't read.

9. **Studio Giraffe Venue Architecture.** Multi-layer event model, promoter ecosystem, revenue stack — all well-structured.

10. **The Clear Glass / Non-Sovereign Ethics Framework.** Prevents the education system from becoming ideological. This is what makes LIFE defensible to school districts.

## SECTION 4 — STAFF RESET ROLLOUT PLAN

### Phase 0 — Preparation (Before Any Staff Meeting)

**Documents to finalize:**
1. New Owner's Top Ten (rewritten per Section 1 recommendations) — printed, posted
2. Staff Orientation Walkthrough (DONE — 10 pages, sign-off)
3. Staff Quick Reference (EXISTS — verify alignment with new Top Ten)
4. Reset Announcement Message (new — replaces the soft claim-based message)
5. Technology Governance Rule (new)
6. Purchasing Authority Protocol (new)

**Physical actions:**
- Remove all AnimalCare Software from all devices
- Remove all staff-created checklists, diet sheets, and task lists from all work areas
- Post new Top Ten in every department, break room, and back room
- Post Back Room Authorization Sign (already exists)

### Phase 1 — The Reset Meeting (Day 1)

**Format:** All-hands meeting. Ammon presents. Not a memo. Not an email. Face-to-face.

**Content:**
1. "We're starting over." — Acknowledge the drift directly. Not blame, but facts.
2. "The animal care software is gone." — Software is removed, old system is deleted.
3. "Here is how we operate now." — Walk through the NEW Top Ten (posted on wall behind you).
4. "You will each sit with me individually." — Announce the 1-on-1 walkthrough schedule.

**Duration:** 15-20 minutes. No questions until after individual walkthroughs.

### Phase 2 — Individual Walkthroughs (Days 1-5)

**Format:** Ammon + each staff member, one at a time.

**Document:** Staff Orientation Walkthrough (10 pages)

**Process:**
1. Sit down together
2. Read each page aloud together
3. Answer questions at each page
4. Staff signs page 10
5. Staff receives their copy of the Staff Quick Reference
6. Signed walkthrough goes in their personnel file

**Pace:** ~20 minutes per person. Schedule by department.

### Phase 3 — New Daily Operations Begin (Week 2)

**Changes staff will see:**
- New diet sheets (owner-approved, signed)
- New task posting system (claim-based)
- Photo verification enforced immediately
- Food waste collection begins (Zero Waste loop)
- All purchases require approval

**Manager role:**
- Review all photos daily
- Respond "Verified" or "Correction needed"
- Document all escalation events
- Report non-compliance same day

### Phase 4 — 30-Day Audit (Week 5)

**Review:**
- Photo verification compliance rate
- Food waste weight trend
- Task claim rate vs unclaimed tasks
- Any unauthorized modifications caught
- Staff who have not engaged with the new system

**Decision point:** Any staff member who has not adapted by Day 30 moves to Strike 2 (formal warning + retraining) unless already at Strike 3.

### Phase 5 — Quarterly System Review (Ongoing)

**Every 90 days:**
- Review all recurring tasks — cut busywork
- Review food waste data — is the loop working?
- Review labor hours — is the trend correcting?
- Review any new technology requests
- Review exhibit condition — any unauthorized modifications?
