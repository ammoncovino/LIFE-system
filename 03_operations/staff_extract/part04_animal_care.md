# PART 4 — ANIMAL CARE AND DIET

Animal care is not optional. It is the core obligation of this facility. Every animal depends on us to feed it correctly, house it correctly, and care for it correctly. There are no shortcuts.

---

## Diet Verification Protocol

This is the step-by-step process for every feeding, every time.

### Step 1 — Prepare

Use the written diet sheet. The diet sheet tells you exactly what to prepare, how much, and how. No substitutions. If you run out of an item, you do not replace it with something else. You report it.

### Step 2 — Verify

Before you move forward, confirm the items and quantities against the diet sheet. Check that nothing is missing. Check that the amounts are correct.


### Step 3 — Deliver

Place the food according to the protocol for that species. Follow the delivery instructions on the diet sheet.

### Rules

- No guessing.
- No "close enough."
- If something is wrong: Stop → Correct → Redo.
- No animal fed to fullness before guest hours.

## Manager Sign-Off System

Managers must:
1. Review ALL diet compliance daily.
2. Confirm accuracy.
3. Respond "Verified – [Initials]."

If a feeding is incorrect:
1. Require correction.
2. Document the issue.

**No silent approvals.** If a manager does not respond "Verified," the feeding is not verified. Silence is not acceptance.

---

## Diet Authority

**Diets are written by the owner or owner-designated authority ONLY.**

- No staff member can create or modify diet sheets.
- No software can create or modify diet sheets.
- No vendor can create or modify diet sheets.
- Every diet sheet must be signed and dated by the authority.
- Any diet sheet without an authority signature is void — do not use it.

**Rule:** If a diet sheet does not have the owner's signature, it does not exist.

Animals cannot be moved without upper manager approval. Animals cannot go to vet without upper manager approval.

---

## Feeding Measurements

All feeding quantities use whole items — a papaya (the whole thing), a mango, a count of bananas. No grams. No weight measurements. Cut items in half at most.

---

## Mulberry Branches

Go in a plastic vase, zip-tied tight to exhibit. Water deep. Branches zip-tied so animals cannot pull them out. Stay wet, like a tree to hold on to. Old mulberry branches go to aviaries for birds to make nests and for foraging. Same with bamboo.

---

## Horses

Only fed hay. Guests can feed hay only on slow days. Put bale on ground. Remove hangers everywhere — hay falls, they consider it garbage and throw it away. Just leave hay on ground.

---

## Exhibit Ground Maintenance

Do not rake up poop everywhere. Leave exhibits as natural biome. Ground becomes what it is instead of scraped hard dirt. Let grass grow, water it.

---

## Watering System

Very minimal. Check sensors every couple weeks. 3 minutes of watering, not 20 minutes.

---

## Hay-Eating Animals

Kangaroos, goats, horses, tortoises (hay only), capybaras. Make sure they are NOT getting fed other things by guests.

---

## Guest Feeding of Hay

Hay goes OUTSIDE exhibit, given to guests a little at a time through guest contact.

---

## Water Dishes

Just clean water. Building water is already filtered — any water used is filtered.
