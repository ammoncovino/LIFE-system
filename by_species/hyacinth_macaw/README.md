# Hyacinth Macaw
*Anodorhynchus hyacinthinus*

## LIFE Questions

**What can I see or hear?**

World's largest flying parrot — 3.3 ft long. Cobalt blue with yellow eye-ring. Beak strong enough to crack a coconut — bite force around 600+ PSI, the highest of any bird.

**Where does it live, and why?**

Pantanal wooded savanna. Nests almost exclusively in 60+ year old Manduvi trees. Two palm species feed it: Acrocomia and Attalea — its ecology is wired to those trees.

**How does it send messages?**

Loud rolling calls. Mates preen, feed, and 'kiss' each other. Pairs stay close and call back and forth in flight to keep contact.

**What does it do when something changes?**

Forages palm nuts in trees AND in cattle dung — cattle can't digest the nuts, so the macaw cracks the easier post-cattle shell. Cooperates with rodents that strip outer skin.

**How does it learn?**

Long juvenile period — chicks dependent for over a year. Learn complex feeding technique (some use leaf or wood tools to grip slippery nuts before cracking).

**How does it have babies?**

Pair-bonded for life. Breeds Aug to Nov in the Pantanal. 1 to 2 eggs in a tree hollow, ~28 day incubation. Female incubates, male feeds her. Slow recovery.

**What are the limits?**

Lifespan 30 to 50 years. Endangered — depends on old trees and on two palm species. Lose either, and the bird loses everything. Pet trade pressure remains.

**What does this teach us?**

Specialization is a contract with the environment. This bird trades flexibility for power — and depends on cattle, palms, rodents, and trees older than most people. Connection IS the diet.

## Diet (90/10)

**Diet type:** Palm-nut specialist  ·  Wild-range diet

**Range:** Pantanal and central Brazil, eastern Bolivia, NE Paraguay — wooded savanna, palm groves, gallery forest

**3-Second Check:** Do I see nut — hard, whole, in the shell or shell-cracked?

**The Rule:** High-oil nut specialist. The bite force exists for one reason — the palm nut.

### Base (90%)
- **Palm nuts (Acrocomia, Attalea)** — primary — almost the entire wild diet
- **Other hard nuts (macadamia, brazil, walnut)** — in care, substituting for palm

### Treats (10%, rotated)
- **Soft fruit** — apple, pear, banana — supplement
- **Vegetables** — carrot, cucumber — minor
- **Pomacea snails** — occasional in the wild
