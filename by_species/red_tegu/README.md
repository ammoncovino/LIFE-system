# Red Tegu
*Salvator rufescens*

## LIFE Questions

**What can I see or hear?**

A reddish, heavy-bodied lizard up to 4 to 5 feet. Forked tongue flicks the air. Powerful jaws, blunt teeth for crushing. Diurnal — active by day, basking in sun.

**Where does it live, and why?**

Temperate Chaco and Espinal of central South America. Burrows by habit. Brumates underground for up to 7 months in cold periods. Burrows are thermal refuge.

**How does it send messages?**

Body posture, tongue-flicks (reading scent), tail movement. Hisses and puffs when threatened. Recognizes individual handlers by scent and sight.

**What does it do when something changes?**

Brumates — buries in and stays largely dormant through cold months. During reproductive season, raises body temperature several degrees ABOVE its burrow.

**How does it learn?**

Highly intelligent for a reptile — solves problems, recognizes people, learns target-and-reward training. Investigates new objects with the tongue.

**How does it have babies?**

Female builds a nest, lays around 30 eggs, and aggressively guards it. Eggs hatch in 120 to 170 days. Hatchlings are 8 inches long and self-sufficient at hatch.

**What are the limits?**

Lifespan 15 to 20 years. Cold below ~50°F shuts them down. Cannot tolerate captivity without burrows, basking heat, and varied protein.

**What does this teach us?**

A cold-blooded animal that bends the rules — generates its own heat to protect eggs. Animals are not locked into one rulebook; environment selects for whatever works.

## Diet (90/10)

**Diet type:** Omnivore  ·  Wild-range diet

**Range:** Western Argentina, Bolivia, Paraguay — Chaco scrub and savanna edge

**3-Second Check:** Do I see protein — visible in the bowl?

**The Rule:** Opportunistic omnivore — animal protein dominant, fruit and plants seasonal.

### Base (90%)
- **Insects & small prey** — primary protein — opportunistic forager
- **Fruit & vegetable matter** — seasonal — fallen fruit, berries, plants

### Treats (10%, rotated)
- **Eggs** — ground-nest opportunism
- **Small rodents / birds / fish** — scavenged or hunted opportunistically
- **Carrion** — any dead animal in their range
