# Dwarf Caiman
*Paleosuchus palpebrosus*

## LIFE Questions

**What can I see or hear?**

Smallest living crocodilian — 4 to 5 ft adult. Heavily armored skin with bony osteoderms. Vertical pupils for night vision. Eyes and nostrils on top of head — can breathe and watch while submerged.

**Where does it live, and why?**

Clear, fast-moving forest streams and flooded forests. Burrows up to 11 ft long, often between tree roots. Tolerates cooler water than other caimans — can survive ~6°C.

**How does it send messages?**

Sounds, postures, head-slapping, jaw-clapping. Males make a grunt 'chumph' call during courtship. Hatchlings vocalize from inside the egg — mother responds and digs them out.

**What does it do when something changes?**

Mostly nocturnal. When threatened, inflates body and hisses. Closes nostrils, throat, and windpipe to dive. Estivates in burrows during dry season at ~22°C.

**How does it learn?**

Communication starts inside the egg. Recognizes status displays, holds territory, learns burrow routes. Far less studied than larger crocodilians.

**How does it have babies?**

Female builds a vegetation-and-mud mound, lays 10 to 25 eggs. ~90 day incubation, sex set by nest temperature. Mother stays with young up to 21 months — long for a reptile.

**What are the limits?**

Lifespan 30 to 60 years. Skin too small and bony for leather trade — that's helped them survive. Loses heat fast in cold water; cannot tolerate brackish water.

**What does this teach us?**

Armor isn't only physical. This animal's body, behavior, and mother's care are all layers of protection. The smallest crocodilian has lasted by being well-defended, not big.

## Diet (90/10)

**Diet type:** Carnivore  ·  Wild-range diet

**Range:** Northern South America — Amazon and Orinoco basins — clean fast freshwater streams, flooded forest

**3-Second Check:** Do I see protein — fish or whole prey item?

**The Rule:** Generalist carnivore — fish-led adult diet, invertebrates dominate juvenile diet.

### Base (90%)
- **Fish** — primary adult protein
- **Aquatic invertebrates** — crabs, shrimp, mollusks — major share for juveniles

### Treats (10%, rotated)
- **Small mammals & birds** — opportunistic
- **Frogs, snakes, small reptiles** — wild-range
- **Insects (especially beetles)** — juveniles forage these heavily
