# RED-FOOT TORTOISE

> [!info] Quick Card
> **Scientific:** *Chelonoidis carbonarius*
> **Range:** Forest edges and savannahs of northern South America
> **Trophic:** FOREST OMNIVORE — FRUIT-DOMINANT

## 3-Second Check
Do I see fallen fruit + greens + a trace of protein?

## The Rule
Forest-edge tortoise. Eats more fruit than greens. Needs a small amount of animal protein WEEKLY — not daily.

## 55 / 35 / 10 Diet (per wild observation)

### Fruit — 55%
- **PAPAYA, MANGO, MELON, BERRIES, BANANA** — fallen-fruit equivalents from the wild
- **MULBERRY, FIG** — soft tropical fruits

### Greens — 35%
- **COLLARD, MUSTARD, DANDELION, ENDIVE, KALE** — rotated, never the same green every day
- **HIBISCUS FLOWERS, ROSE PETALS** — wild forage

### Animal Protein — 10% (ONCE PER WEEK ONLY)
- **EARTHWORM, BOILED EGG, SNAIL** — preferred forms
- **LOW-FAT SOAKED CAT KIBBLE** — backup, sparingly

Plus other plants and prey native to its wild range.

## Taboo — NEVER
No citrus. No dog food. No daily protein. No iceberg lettuce. No grass-only diet (cannot metabolize silica-rich grass).

## How We Feed
- Match the bowl. Don't interpret.
- If the bowl is wrong, escalate: *"This looked like we should take it out. What would you like to do here?"*
- Hold the line. Manager on duty makes the call.

## Why It Matters
The red-foot is a forest-edge omnivore from northern South America. It evolved on fallen tropical fruit, scavenged carrion, and slow-moving invertebrates. A diet stripped of animal protein causes hind-leg paralysis and infertility. A diet stripped of fruit causes the same digestive failure as feeding a sulcata fruit — same family, opposite plate.
