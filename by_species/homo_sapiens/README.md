# Homo Sapiens
*Homo sapiens*

## LIFE Questions

**What can I see or hear?**

Five primary senses (sight, hearing, smell, taste, touch) plus balance, body position, pain, temperature. Discriminates around 1 trillion smells and tens of thousands of color shades.

**Where does it live, and why?**

Every habitat on Earth. The species' adaptation is not to one environment — it's the ABILITY to switch environments. Tools, fire, clothing, and culture extend the body's range.

**How does it send messages?**

Spoken language, writing, gesture, expression, art, music. Language is learned, dialects regional, meaning shared. We talk to people who aren't here and people who haven't been born.

**What does it do when something changes?**

Builds tools, changes the surroundings instead of moving. Stores food, lights fire, cooks, transports water. Group response — not just individual.

**How does it learn?**

Long childhood — 12+ years of dependence. Learns by watching, listening, copying, asking, and being taught. Most knowledge is inherited socially, not biologically.

**How does it have babies?**

Singletons every few years. Both parents and often grandparents help raise young. Young are altricial — helpless at birth, walking at ~1 year, fully grown at ~18+ years.

**What are the limits?**

Lifespan 70 to 85 years typical. We are physically slow, weak, and bare-skinned. The body alone cannot survive most of the places this species now lives — it depends on tools and other people.

**What does this teach us?**

We are the same kind of animal as everything else here. Our advantage is connection — we cooperate, teach, and remember. Take that away and we're naked apes.

## Diet (90/10)

**Diet type:** Omnivore  ·  Generalist

**Range:** All continents — rainforest, desert, tundra, mountain, coast — the only mammal that lives everywhere

**3-Second Check:** Look in the mirror. Are you eating what your environment actually offered today?

**The Rule:** Omnivore generalist — flexibility is the diet. We eat whatever the environment offers and remember how to do it.

### Base (90%)
- **Plant foods** — grains, fruit, vegetables, nuts, legumes — bulk of nearly every traditional diet
- **Animal foods** — meat, fish, eggs, dairy where culturally available

### Treats (10%, rotated)
- **Cooked food** — fire-processing unlocked starch and protein no other ape eats
- **Fermented and stored food** — extended access through seasons
- **Salt, spice, sweet** — preference shaped by ancient scarcity
