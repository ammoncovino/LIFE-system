# Caiman Lizard
*Dracaena guianensis*

## LIFE Questions

**What can I see or hear?**

Heavy-bodied, semi-aquatic lizard with crocodile-like scaled back. Powerful jaws, short round teeth for crushing shells. Forked tongue. Strong swimmer, climbs branches over water.

**Where does it live, and why?**

Flooded woodlands and riverbanks of the Amazon Basin. Splits time between water (hunting, escape) and overhanging branches (basking, sleeping).

**How does it send messages?**

Bright body color signals between males and females. Forked tongue collects scent particles to locate prey, mates, and predators. Posturing in displays.

**What does it do when something changes?**

Dives readily when disturbed. Forages along banks during the day, hides in trees and bushes at night. Body adapts for life on land AND in water.

**How does it learn?**

Intelligent — observed solving problems, recognizing keepers, working out how to dislodge a snail clinging to rocks.

**How does it have babies?**

Female lays 5 to 10 eggs in a riverbank hole, covers them, leaves. ~179 day incubation. Hatchlings emerge fully independent — walk and swim within hours.

**What are the limits?**

Lifespan 10 to 12+ years in care. Specialized diet — wild-caught adults often refuse anything but snails. Needs warm humid forest with deep water access.

**What does this teach us?**

Specialists are powerful but fragile. The same jaws that crush shells lock this animal to a single food. Every strength has a cost.

## Diet (90/10)

**Diet type:** Carnivore (snail specialist)  ·  Wild-range diet

**Range:** Amazon Basin — flooded forest, swamps, riverbanks

**3-Second Check:** Do I see shell — snails or crustacean shell visible?

**The Rule:** Snail and shellfish specialist — calcium comes from the shell, not from a supplement.

### Base (90%)
- **Aquatic snails** — primary food — shells crushed whole, calcium source
- **Crustaceans (crawfish, freshwater clams, crabs)** — secondary protein

### Treats (10%, rotated)
- **Insects** — occasional — crickets, dubia roaches
- **Small fish** — opportunistic
- **Fruits & vegetables** — minor — supplemental only
