# LEOPARD TORTOISE

> [!info] Quick Card
> **Scientific:** *Stigmochelys pardalis*
> **Range:** Savannas of eastern and southern Africa
> **Trophic:** SAVANNA GRAZER — GRASSES + SUCCULENTS

## 3-Second Check
Is the bowl 70%+ grasses/hay + greens + succulents, no fruit?

## The Rule
Savanna grazer. Eats grasses and succulents. NO animal protein. Fruit avoided in captivity — leopards in the wild eat fruit only opportunistically.

## 70 / 30 Diet (per wild observation)

### Grasses, Hay, Mixed Browse — 70%+
- **TIMOTHY, ORCHARD, BERMUDA HAY** — staple grazing base
- **FRESH GRASSES** — when in outdoor enclosure
- **WILD WEEDS** — dandelion, plantain, mulberry, grape leaves

### Greens + Succulents + Forage — ~30%
- **COLLARD, MUSTARD, TURNIP GREENS, ENDIVE, CHICORY** — rotated
- **PRICKLY PEAR PAD + FLOWERS** — desert browse
- **HIBISCUS FLOWERS, ROSE PETALS** — wild forage
- **SQUASH, SWEET POTATO** — sparingly

Plus other plants and prey native to its wild range.

## Taboo — NEVER
No animal protein. No fruit (causes diarrhea and gut imbalance — leopards lack the gut flora of frugivorous species). No iceberg lettuce. No daily commercial pellets as base. No high-protein feed (causes pyramiding).

## How We Feed
- Match the bowl. Don't interpret.
- If the bowl is wrong, escalate: *"This looked like we should take it out. What would you like to do here?"*
- Hold the line. Manager on duty makes the call.

## Why It Matters
The leopard tortoise is a savanna grazer. Wild-diet studies put grasses at over 70% of intake. Captive leopards fed fruit develop diarrhea and gut imbalance — they evolved on a low-sugar, high-fiber diet from semi-arid grasslands. They are similar to sulcatas but accept slightly more variety in greens.

## Leopard vs. Sulcata — Side-by-Side
| | Leopard | Sulcata |
|---|---|---|
| Base | Grasses (70%) + greens (30%) | **Hay only (90%)** |
| Greens variety | Higher | Minimal (10% wild forage) |
| Fruit | Avoided | **NEVER** |
| Protein | NEVER | NEVER |
| Habitat | African savanna | Sahel desert |
