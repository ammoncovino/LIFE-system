# Scarlet Macaw
*Ara macao*

## LIFE Questions

**What can I see or hear?**

~3 ft long bird, half of that is tail. Red, yellow, blue plumage. Hooked beak strong enough to crack the toughest nuts. Loud calls carry several miles.

**Where does it live, and why?**

Rainforest canopy, usually >10 m above the ground — food and safety are up high. Visits exposed riverbank clay licks for minerals and to neutralize plant toxins.

**How does it send messages?**

Loud squawks and screeches keep flocks in contact. Each bird has a unique signature call — a vocal fingerprint. Different alarm calls for aerial vs. ground threats. Mimics ambient sounds.

**What does it do when something changes?**

Flocks of 10 to 30 sleep together at night, fly together to feed. Eats unripe fruit other animals can't open — gains a competitive edge. Eats clay to neutralize toxins.

**How does it learn?**

Vocal learner — develops local 'flock dialects' (regional accents) through social practice. Young stay with parents up to a year, learning food sources and calls.

**How does it have babies?**

Monogamous for life. Lay 2 to 4 eggs in a tree-cavity nest. Female incubates ~25 days, both parents feed the chick. Fledges at ~90 days, independent at ~1 year.

**What are the limits?**

Lifespan 40 to 50 years wild, up to 75 in captivity. Habitat loss + pet trade pressure. Low reproductive rate — losing one breeding tree affects many years of young.

**What does this teach us?**

Language is a social skill, not just a sound. These birds learn names, accents, and warnings from their flock. Culture isn't only human.

## Diet (90/10)

**Diet type:** Frugivore / granivore  ·  Wild-range diet

**Range:** Southern Mexico, Central America, Amazon Basin — rainforest canopy below 1,000 m

**3-Second Check:** Do I see nut or whole seed — not just pellet?

**The Rule:** Hard nuts and tropical fruit. The clay isn't garnish — it's a digestive tool.

### Base (90%)
- **Tree nuts and seeds** — primary — beak built for crushing hardest nuts
- **Tropical fruit** — ripe and unripe — broad palette

### Treats (10%, rotated)
- **Flowers and nectar** — occasional
- **Clay from mineral licks** — for sodium, calcium, toxin-binding
- **Leaves and bark** — minor browse
