# YELLOW-FOOT TORTOISE

> [!info] Quick Card
> **Scientific:** *Chelonoidis denticulatus*
> **Range:** Amazon basin and northern South American rainforests
> **Trophic:** DEEP-RAINFOREST OMNIVORE — GREENS-DOMINANT

## 3-Second Check
Do I see leafy greens + a little fruit + a trace of protein?

## The Rule
Deep-rainforest tortoise. Eats more greens than fruit — opposite of the red-foot. Trace animal protein weekly.

## 75 / 20 / 5 Diet (per wild observation)

### Greens, Herbs, Browse — 75–80%
- **COLLARD, DANDELION, MUSTARD, ENDIVE** — rotated daily
- **MULBERRY LEAVES, PLANTAIN, HIBISCUS, ROSE PETALS** — wild forage equivalents
- **GRASSES + BROWSE** — when seasonally available

### Fruit + Mushrooms — ~20%
- **PAPAYA, MELON, BERRIES, MANGO** — sparingly
- **EDIBLE MUSHROOMS** — once a month max (mineral-rich but high-purine)

### Animal Protein — ~5% (ONCE PER WEEK ONLY)
- **SNAIL, EARTHWORM, HORNWORM** — preferred forms
- **BOILED EGG** — backup

Plus other plants and prey native to its wild range.

## Taboo — NEVER
No citrus. No dog food. No daily protein. No daily fruit (causes digestive imbalance — yellow-foots eat much less fruit than red-foots in the wild).

## How We Feed
- Match the bowl. Don't interpret.
- If the bowl is wrong, escalate: *"This looked like we should take it out. What would you like to do here?"*
- Hold the line. Manager on duty makes the call.

## Why It Matters
The yellow-foot is a deep-rainforest tortoise. Field studies show 75–80% of its wild intake is herbs, leaves, flowers, and grasses. Fruit is occasional — fallen, opportunistic, not a daily staple. Treating a yellow-foot like a red-foot (fruit-dominant) is a husbandry error in the opposite direction from treating it like a sulcata.

## Red-Foot vs. Yellow-Foot — Side-by-Side
| | Red-foot | Yellow-foot |
|---|---|---|
| Greens | ~35% | **~75%** |
| Fruit | **~55%** | ~20% |
| Protein | ~10% | ~5% |
| Habitat | Forest-edge | Deep rainforest |
