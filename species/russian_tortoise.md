# RUSSIAN TORTOISE

> [!info] Quick Card
> **Scientific:** *Testudo horsfieldii*
> **Range:** Central Asian dry steppe (Russia, Iran, Afghanistan, Pakistan, China)
> **Trophic:** STEPPE GRAZER — GREENS + WEEDS

## 3-Second Check
Is the bowl 90% leafy greens + weeds, no fruit, no protein?

## The Rule
Dry-steppe herbivore. Eats LEAFY GREENS, NOT GRASS HAY (different from sulcata). NO animal protein. Fruit is rare — once a week max, if at all.

## 90 / 10 Diet (per wild observation)

### Leafy Greens, Weeds, Wild Forage — 90%
- **DANDELION GREENS** — preferred staple
- **COLLARD, MUSTARD, TURNIP GREENS, KALE** — rotated daily
- **ENDIVE, ESCAROLE, CHICORY** — high-fiber, low-protein
- **GRAPE LEAVES, MULBERRY LEAVES, PLANTAIN** — wild forage

### Edible Flowers + Vegetables — ~10% (rotated)
- **HIBISCUS, ROSE PETALS, NASTURTIUM, CLOVER** — wild flowers
- **PRICKLY PEAR PAD** — desert browse
- **CARROT, SQUASH, SWEET POTATO** — sparingly

Plus other plants and prey native to its wild range.

## Taboo — NEVER
No animal protein (Russians DO NOT eat meat). No iceberg lettuce. No spinach as a staple (oxalate). No bell peppers or tomato. Fruit is rare — berries or melon a few times a year only. No commercial pellets as base.

## How We Feed
- Match the bowl. Don't interpret.
- If the bowl is wrong, escalate: *"This looked like we should take it out. What would you like to do here?"*
- Hold the line. Manager on duty makes the call.

## Why It Matters
The Russian tortoise is a high-elevation steppe grazer with the northernmost range of any tortoise. It evolved on weeds, dandelions, and wild greens — not grass hay (sulcata) and not fruit (red-foot). Feeding fruit causes diarrhea and gut imbalance. Feeding protein causes shell deformity and kidney disease. This is a leafy-green tortoise, period.

## Russian vs. Sulcata — Side-by-Side
| | Russian | Sulcata |
|---|---|---|
| Base | Leafy greens (90%) | **Hay only (90%)** |
| Fruit | Rare | **NEVER** |
| Protein | NEVER | NEVER |
| Habitat | Dry steppe | Sahel desert |
