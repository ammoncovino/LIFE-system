## System Overview — LIFE

**LIFE:** Language, Intelligence, Form, Ecology.

**Purpose:**
A non-ideological, mechanism-first education system designed to:
- Teach how systems function
- Preserve human agency
- Avoid persuasion, ideology, and moral imposition
- Enable observation-first learning
- Scale across children, adults, and staff

## Epistemic Gating (Layer VI)

**Definition:** Controlled exposure to information based on cognitive readiness.

- **Children's Lens:** observation-first, no ideology, no burden
- **Adult Lens:** may destabilize beliefs, requires voluntary engagement
- **Staff Lens:** learning through building, accountability through ownership

---

## Education-by-Construction (Layer VII)

**Definition:** Learning method where participants build the system and knowledge emerges through creation. Not lecture-based or passive instruction.

**Structure:** Build → Observe → Correct → Repeat

**Components:**
- Staff creates materials
- Errors are visible
- Corrections are public
- Learning emerges through iteration

**Rule:** Passive learning is not allowed.

---

## Structural vs Transmission Model

Structure equals truth. Transmission equals delivery. Structure is fixed, non-negotiable, system-based. Transmission is flexible, audience-dependent, replaceable. If conflict occurs, structure overrides transmission.

---

## Lens Mapping Model

Structural lenses include Clear Glass (ethical constraint) and Alpha–Omega (failure cycle). Transmission lenses include inversion, inverted nuns, two samurai, and fairy tales. Rule: Transmission requires structure. Structure does not require transmission.

## AI Interpretive Model

**Core flow:** Observation → Question → AI → Explanation → Reflection.

**Rules:**
- AI responds after observation
- AI explains mechanisms
- AI does not determine meaning

**Failure conditions:** If AI persuades, moralizes, or replaces human judgment, it violates the system.

## Micro-Sign System

**Purpose:** Replace TVs, long signage, and staff explanation with minimal prompts enabling inquiry.

**Core Pattern:** Observe first. Then ask your AI.

**Standard Prompt:** "What system is this, and what does it need to work?"

**Variants:**
- What makes this possible?
- What makes this impossible?
- What changes if one part is removed?

**Micro-Sign System Model:**

Structure: Each zone contains an observation cue, a core question, and optional prompts. Core question: What system is this, and what does it require to function? Optional prompts: What makes this possible? What makes this impossible? What changes if one part is removed? Rule: Minimal input produces maximal inquiry.

**Primary Sign Text:**
Observe first. Then ask your AI. "What system is this, and what does it need to work?"

**Variants:** What makes this possible? What makes this impossible? What changes if one part is removed?
