# Mirror, Mirror — Illusion Suite
## Wall Content for Handwritten Calligraphy and Canvas Pieces

**Governing Statement:** Perception does not equal reality.

**Suite Position:** Phase 1, Suites 1 + 3 (Reflection + Illusion)
**Animals Present:** Lemurs (behind curtains), Cats (porch view window)
**Physical Elements:** Black curtains hiding exhibits, bathroom mirror, elevated treehouse visible from exhibit floor below, sunset/cloud mural, rope bridge entry

---

## CATEGORY 1 — Opening Questions (Large Canvas, Near Entry or Bed)

These go where someone sees them first — when they walk in or when they lie down.

1. **What did you see on the way in — and what did you miss?**

2. **Are you inside the exhibit, or is the exhibit inside you?**

3. **Who is watching whom?**

---

## CATEGORY 2 — Curtain Questions (Near or On the Black Curtains)

These should be visible BEFORE the guest pulls the curtain back. The reveal is the lesson.

4. **What is behind this curtain that you cannot see?**

5. **If something is hidden, does it stop being real?**

6. **Pull this aside. What you find was here the entire time.**

7. **The curtain did not create the separation. You assumed it.**

---

## CATEGORY 3 — Mirror / Bathroom Wall (Near the Mirror)

The mirror itself becomes the lesson. These go near or beside the bathroom mirror.

8. **Mirror, mirror — what you see is not what is there.**

9. **A mirror shows your surface. What does it miss?**

10. **The lemur sees you before you see it. What does that tell you about your senses?**

11. **You trust your eyes. They are the most limited tool you own.**

---

## CATEGORY 4 — Observation Window / Porch (Cat Viewing Area)

The outdoor porch with the view window into the cat exhibit. Guests sit and watch.

12. **You are sitting still. The cat is deciding whether you matter.**

13. **The glass makes you feel safe. The cat does not need the glass.**

14. **What is the cat watching while you watch it?**

15. **You came to observe. You are being observed.**

---

## CATEGORY 5 — Contemplation Pieces (Wall Near Bed, Ceiling-Facing, or Open Wall Space)

These are for lying in bed, looking at the mural, being still. The space forces contemplation.

16. **This room is inside an animal exhibit. You paid to sleep where they live. What does that make you?**

17. **Every animal in this building solved the problem of survival. You solved the problem of comfort. Which one required more intelligence?**

18. **The clouds on this wall are painted. The animal behind the curtain is real. Your brain treats both as scenery.**

19. **Tonight you will sleep ten feet from a lemur. It will not sleep because you are here. One of you is adapted to this situation.**

20. **You are inside a system you did not build, surrounded by organisms you did not design, breathing air you did not create. You are a guest in every sense of the word.**

---

## CATEGORY 6 — Illusion / Things-Are-Not-What-They-Seem (Anywhere in Suite)

Pure illusion-themed content. These can go on any open wall, canvas, or surface.

21. **Things are not as they seem. They never were.**

22. **What looks like a room is an exhibit. What looks like an exhibit is a habitat. What looks like a habitat is a world you were not invited into.**

23. **A lemur's nose tells it more about you than your eyes tell you about it.**

24. **Camouflage is not deception. It is design. The animal is not hiding from you. You were never the audience.**

25. **The most dangerous illusion is the one you do not know you believe.**

26. **This building has no outside. Everything you see is inside something else.**

---

## CATEGORY 7 — Short Statements for Small Canvases or Cards

Quick-hit pieces. One line. Good for small canvases, propped on shelves, or placed on nightstands.

27. **What you see is partial.**

28. **Perception is not truth.**

29. **The animal is not performing.**

30. **Comfort is the deepest illusion.**

31. **You are inside the exhibit.**

32. **Nothing here is decoration.**

33. **The curtain is the lesson.**

34. **What you missed matters more than what you noticed.**

35. **The mirror shows you. The lemur sees through you.**

---

## PLACEMENT NOTES

- **Entry wall (barn door side):** #1 or #2 — large canvas, first thing they read
- **Curtain panels (on or beside each curtain):** #4, #5, #6, #7
- **Bathroom mirror area:** #8, #9, #10
- **Porch / cat view window:** #12, #13, #14, #15
- **Above or beside bed (mural wall):** #16, #17, #18, #19, #20
- **Open wood wall surfaces:** #21–#26 distributed across available space
- **Small canvases / nightstand / shelf:** #27–#35

---

## FORMAT GUIDANCE FOR CALLIGRAPHY

- **Questions** work best in a slightly lighter hand — they should feel like invitations, not commands
- **Statements** (#21–#35) can be bolder — these are the system talking
- The wood walls and warm tones of the suite favor dark ink (black, deep brown, or navy) on light or natural canvas
- The mural wall (orange/sunset) favors white or cream canvas with dark text
- Keep it readable from the bed — if someone is lying down, the text above them should be legible from 4–6 feet
